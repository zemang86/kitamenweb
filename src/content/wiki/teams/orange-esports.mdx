## Who were Orange eSports?

**Orange eSports** were a Malaysian **[Dota 2](/wiki/games/dota-2)** organisation best
remembered for a landmark run at **[The International 2013 (TI3)](/wiki/tournaments/the-international)**,
where their all-Malaysian roster finished **3rd** — at the time the **best-ever TI
placement and highest earnings for a Southeast Asian team** (~US$287,441).

## The TI3 roster

The bronze-medal lineup was **[Mushi](/wiki/players/mushi)** (Chai Yee Fung), **Ohaiyo**
(Khoo Chong Xin), **kYxY** (Lee Kong Yang), **XtiNcT** (Joel Chan Zhan Leong) and **Net**
(Lim Wai Pern) — all Malaysian.

## Legacy

That core seeded the **Team Malaysia** project (2014–15), which earned Southeast Asia's
only direct invite to TI 2015 and was then signed by **Fnatic**, beginning the well-known
Malaysian-core Fnatic era. Orange eSports' TI3 bronze remains a touchstone for Malaysian
Dota 2.
