## What is iRacing?

**iRacing** is a **subscription-based, online-only PC racing simulator** operated by
**iRacing.com Motorsport Simulations**, launched in **2008**. It is known for its serious,
results-driven competition: a strict licensing and safety-rating system, laser-scanned
tracks, and a deep calendar of official championships.

## Official championships

iRacing runs several flagship series, including the **eNASCAR Coca-Cola iRacing Series**,
the **Porsche Esports Supercup** and the **IMSA iRacing Pro Series**. In 2025, **Steven
Wilson** took his second eNASCAR title and **Cooper Webster** won the Porsche Esports
Supercup.

## iRacing and Malaysia's sim-to-real pipeline

iRacing is central to Malaysia's clearest **sim-to-real story**: brothers
**[Naquib](/wiki/players/naquib-azlan)** and **[Nabil Azlan](/wiki/players/nabil-azlan)**
made their names on iRacing, were spotted through
**[Alex Yoong's](/wiki/players/alex-yoong)** eRacing events, and graduated to real-world
racing — each going on to win the **Sepang 1000km**.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is iRacing free?

No — iRacing is subscription-based, and most cars and tracks are bought as content. It is
PC-only and played entirely online.

### Why is iRacing important to Malaysian sim racing?

It is where Malaysia's most prominent sim-to-real drivers, the Azlan brothers, built the
skills that took them into professional motorsport.
