## What is EA SPORTS FC?

**EA SPORTS FC** is the **football (soccer) simulation** developed by **EA Vancouver
and EA Romania** and published by **Electronic Arts**. It is the direct successor to
the **FIFA** series: after EA's licensing deal with FIFA ended, **FC 24 (2023)**
became the first title to ship without the FIFA name. The current release is
**EA SPORTS FC 26**, launched **26 September 2025**.

Competitive EA FC is played **1v1** in the **[Ultimate Team (FUT)](/wiki/glossary/ultimate-team)**
mode on EA's own **FC Pro** circuit.

## EA FC is not eFootball (or "FIFAe")

A common mix-up: since the 2022 split, **EA runs its own competitive circuit (FC Pro)**,
while the separate **"FIFAe"** brand now runs **Konami's eFootball**, Rocket League and
Football Manager. They are different games. Malaysia's biggest football-esports result —
Muhaimin "Minbappe" Razak's FIFAe world title — was won in **eFootball**, *not* EA FC.

## EA FC in Malaysia

Malaysia competes in EA's **"Asia South"** region (Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia, the
Philippines, Thailand, Vietnam and India). The scene is modest and regional — no
Malaysian has reached the **[FC Pro World Championship](/wiki/tournaments/fc-pro-world-championship)** —
but there is a real competitive pathway, and **[Rajabos](/wiki/players/rajabos)** is the
standout local name, having won an FC Pro Asia South regional cup. As a console title,
EA FC is also a staple of Malaysian event circuits run by organisers like
**[Esports Integrated](/wiki/orgs/esports-integrated)**.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is EA SPORTS FC the same as FIFA?

It is the successor. EA made the FIFA games until FIFA 23; after the licensing split,
the series continued as EA SPORTS FC from FC 24 (2023) onward, keeping the same league
and club licences but dropping the FIFA name.

### How do Malaysians compete in EA FC?

Through EA's FC Pro circuit in the "Asia South" region — open ladders and regional
qualifiers that feed toward the FC Pro World Championship.
