## What is Mobile Legends: Bang Bang?

**Mobile Legends: Bang Bang (MLBB)** is a 5v5 multiplayer online battle arena
(MOBA) developed by **Moonton** for mobile devices. Two teams of five players
push lanes, contest objectives, and aim to destroy the enemy base. Matches are
short — typically 10–20 minutes — which suits mobile-first audiences and is a
big reason the title dominates Southeast Asian esports.

## Why it matters in Malaysia

MLBB is the **largest competitive esports title in Malaysia** by player base,
viewership, and prize money. The local professional scene is anchored by
[MPL Malaysia](/wiki/tournaments/mpl-malaysia), the franchised league that feeds
into regional and world championships.

## Scale and live game state

MLBB reports **110M+ monthly active users** and **1.5B+ installs** (Moonton).
The roster reached **133 heroes** as of June 2026, the newest being **Hirara**,
an assassin jungler released on 17 June 2026 alongside **Season 41 "Scarlet
Embers"** (patch 2.1.88). Hero counts and the meta shift roughly every six weeks,
so always check the current patch.

## The five roles

Competitive MLBB is built around five lane/role assignments:

- **[Gold Laner](/wiki/glossary/gold-laner)** — the team's primary marksman and late-game damage dealer.
- **EXP Laner** — a durable fighter who holds the side lane.
- **Mid Laner** — a flexible mage or burst hero controlling the centre.
- **[Jungler](/wiki/glossary/jungler)** — clears neutral camps for gold and XP, then roams to fight.
- **[Roamer](/wiki/glossary/roamer)** — a tank or support who protects the core and sets up picks.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is Mobile Legends free to play?

Yes. MLBB is free to download and play; heroes and cosmetics can be earned
in-game or bought.

### How long is a competitive match?

Most games run 10–20 minutes, with professional series played as best-of-three
or best-of-five.
