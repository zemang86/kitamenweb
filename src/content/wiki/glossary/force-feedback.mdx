## Force feedback — definition

**Force feedback (FFB)** is the **motorised resistance and vibration** a
**[sim racing](/wiki/glossary/sim-racing)** wheel produces. It is how the simulator
communicates what the car is doing — the loading of the front tyres in a corner, the rumble of
a kerb, the moment grip breaks into a slide — so the driver can react by feel rather than by
sight alone.

Entry wheels such as **Logitech G's** G29/G920 use **gear-driven** FFB, while serious rigs use
**[direct-drive](/wiki/glossary/direct-drive)** wheelbases for stronger, more detailed feel.
Good FFB is what separates a realistic simulator from an arcade racer.
