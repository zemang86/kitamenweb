## Load-cell pedals — definition

**Load-cell pedals** measure the brake by **how hard you press** rather than **how far you
push** the pedal. A sensor reads the applied **force/pressure** — exactly like a real car's
brake — instead of measuring travel as basic potentiometer pedals do.

The payoff is **consistency**: because you brake to a feel of pressure, you can hit the same
braking point lap after lap, which is essential for competitive
**[sim racing](/wiki/glossary/sim-racing)**. A load-cell brake is one of the most impactful
upgrades a sim racer can make, alongside a **[direct-drive](/wiki/glossary/direct-drive)**
wheelbase.
