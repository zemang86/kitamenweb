## Racing line — definition

The **racing line** is the **fastest path through a corner** — the geometry of **turn-in →
apex → exit** that carries the most speed. The **apex** is the point where the car runs
closest to the **inside** of the corner; clipping it at the right moment lets the driver
straighten the wheel earlier and get on the throttle sooner for a quicker exit.

A good line is about the **whole sequence**, not one corner in isolation: sacrificing a little
entry speed to nail the exit onto a straight is often faster overall. In
**[sim racing](/wiki/glossary/sim-racing)**, drivers refine the line using
**[telemetry](/wiki/glossary/telemetry)** to compare laps corner by corner.
