## Car setup — definition

A **car setup** is the bundle of **tunable parameters** a sim racer adjusts to suit a track and
their driving style: **suspension stiffness, ride height, tyre pressures, gear ratios,
aerodynamic downforce, brake bias and the differential**, among others.

A good setup balances competing demands — grip versus tyre wear, stability versus agility,
straight-line speed versus cornering — and is tuned to each circuit. Sim racers use
**[telemetry](/wiki/glossary/telemetry)** to confirm a change actually made the car faster.
(Setup is distinct from **[Balance of Performance](/wiki/glossary/balance-of-performance)**,
which is set by organisers to equalise different cars, not by the driver.)
