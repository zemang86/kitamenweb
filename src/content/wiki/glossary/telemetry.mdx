## Telemetry — definition

**Telemetry** is the **recorded performance data** from a lap — **speed, throttle, brake,
steering angle, g-forces, gear, and temperatures** — logged so it can be studied afterwards.
It turns "the car felt loose there" into something measurable.

In **[sim racing](/wiki/glossary/sim-racing)**, drivers and coaches overlay two laps' telemetry
to see exactly where time is gained or lost: braking a few metres too early, missing the
**[apex](/wiki/glossary/racing-line)**, or getting on the throttle late. It is also how a
**[car setup](/wiki/glossary/setup)** is validated — proving a change actually made the car
faster, not just different.
